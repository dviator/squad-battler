# Single Starting Ability with Progression to More

Units currently start with too many skills/abilities at once. It's too much to
take in at the start of the game and makes it hard to understand what's going on.
Pare this down: units should begin with just **one ability** and progress to more
over time.

This is an onboarding/legibility concern — lower the early-game cognitive load so a
player can read and understand a battle before complexity ramps up. Unlocking more
abilities should tie into the existing genetic/roguelike progression (leveling,
breeding, mutations, or run-stage milestones).

## Open questions (for refinement)

- What triggers ability unlocks — level thresholds, mutations, breeding, or run-stage milestones?
- Is the starting single ability fixed per species, or chosen/randomized?
- Strip abilities at species-definition time, or gate them at runtime?
- Max abilities a unit can reach, and over what timeframe?
- Show "locked" ability slots so progression feels rewarding and legible?
